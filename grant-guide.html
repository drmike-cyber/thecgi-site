What Is WIOA? — The Federal Law Behind the Funding
Federal Law

The Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA) is a federal law signed by President Obama in 2014 — bipartisan legislation that reauthorized and improved America's public workforce system. It is the primary federal law funding job training and workforce development programs across all 50 states.

WIOA is not a new program or a temporary stimulus. It has been the law of the land for over a decade, administering billions of dollars annually through state workforce agencies, local CareerSource offices, and approved training providers.

📋
Federal Legislation
Public Law 113-128 · Signed July 22, 2014 · Reauthorized the Workforce Investment Act of 1998 · Administered by the U.S. Department of Labor Employment and Training Administration

dol.gov/agencies/eta/wioa
💰
Scale of Funding
WIOA provides approximately $3 billion annually in federal workforce development funding across the United States. Florida alone receives tens of millions of dollars per program year — much of which goes unclaimed by eligible employers.

dol.gov/general/topic/training/wioa

Stream 2 — Individual Training Accounts (ITA)
CareerSource Tampa Bay

In addition to the employer reimbursement pathway, individual employees may qualify for WIOA Individual Training Account (ITA) vouchers — which, for those CareerSource determines eligible, pay the training provider directly and may reduce or eliminate out-of-pocket cost. Eligibility is determined by CareerSource and is not guaranteed.

🏢
Official Source — CareerSource Tampa Bay
CareerSource Tampa Bay administers WIOA Individual Training Accounts for residents of Hillsborough and Pinellas Counties. Once an individual is determined eligible for training services, they may select an approved provider from the Eligible Training Provider List (ETPL) and receive an ITA voucher that covers the cost of training.

The ETPL — What It Is and Why CGI Is Pursuing It
Florida State

The Eligible Training Provider List (ETPL) is the official state-approved list of training institutions that are authorized to receive WIOA funding on behalf of their students and employer-clients. Think of it as the state's seal of approval on a training provider.

For an individual employee's ITA voucher to pay CGI directly, CGI must be on the ETPL. For an employer's IWT reimbursement claim to reference CGI specifically, CGI must be approved by CareerSource Florida as a training provider. Both approvals require the same foundational step: CIE licensure.

⚠️
Important Transparency Notice
CGI's ETPL approval is currently in process. CGI has filed its CIE Provisional License application with the Florida Commission for Independent Education and has initiated the Employ Florida ETPL registration. Companies that enroll in G.A.I.N.S. certification now are positioned as founding clients — first in line for grant reimbursement processing the moment CGI's ETPL approval is granted. The certification itself is fully valid and deliverable today, independent of ETPL status. The grant reimbursement pathway activates upon ETPL approval.
The ETPL Approval Process — Three Official Steps
1
CIE Provisional License — Florida Commission for Independent Education
All private postsecondary training institutions in Florida must be licensed by the Florida Commission for Independent Education before they can operate or be approved as an ETPL provider. This is the foundational requirement. CGI's application is filed and under review.

Official agency: Florida Commission for Independent Education (CIE)
Phone: (850) 245-3200
Email: cieinfo@fldoe.org
Website: fldoe.org/policy/cie
Governing statute: Sections 1005.01–1005.39, Florida Statutes
2
State ETPL Registration — Florida Department of Commerce via Employ Florida
Once CIE-licensed, training providers apply for inclusion on the State ETPL through the Employ Florida portal. As of July 1, 2024, the ETPL application process is managed by Florida Commerce via a statewide process. State ETPL approval is required before applying to any local CareerSource ETPL.

Official portal: employflorida.com → Sign In/Register → Provider
State ETPL Coordinator email: ETPL@commerce.fl.gov
Florida Department of Commerce: floridajobs.org
Governing policy: CareerSource Florida Administrative Policy -090-
3
Local ETPL — CareerSource Tampa Bay (July–September 2026 Window)
Once on the State ETPL, providers apply to the local CareerSource board for inclusion on the local ETPL. The CareerSource Tampa Bay local ETPL application window opens annually in July. State ETPL approval is a prerequisite. Once on the local ETPL, CGI can accept ITA vouchers directly from CareerSource Tampa Bay on behalf of qualifying individual employees.
