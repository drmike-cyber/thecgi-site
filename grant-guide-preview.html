What Is WIOA? — The Federal Law Behind the Funding
Federal Law

The Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA) is a federal law — Public Law 113-128 — that funds job training and workforce development programs across all 50 states. It has been the law of the land since 2014, administering billions of dollars annually. It is not a new program or temporary stimulus.

📋
Federal Legislation
Public Law 113-128 · Signed July 22, 2014 · Administered by the U.S. Department of Labor Employment and Training Administration.

Verify at: dol.gov/agencies/eta/wioa
💰
Two Funding Streams for You
Stream 1 — IWT: Your company pays for training, the state reimburses you up to $3,500 per employee.

Stream 2 — ITA: Individual employees who qualify receive vouchers that pay the training provider directly — zero cost to you or your team.
2
Stream 1 — Incumbent Worker Training (IWT) Grant
Florida Program

The Incumbent Worker Training (IWT) Program is administered by CareerSource Florida and funded under WIOA. It reimburses Florida businesses for the cost of upgrading their current employees' skills.

🏛️
Official Source — CareerSource Florida
The IWT program provides reimbursement grants to businesses that pay for pre-approved, direct, training-related costs. From 2023–2024, CareerSource Florida awarded $2.9 million in IWT grants to 69 businesses, benefiting over 2,700 full-time employees. Since inception, more than 3,100 Florida companies have utilized IWT grants.

Stream 2 — Individual Training Accounts (ITA)

In addition to the employer reimbursement pathway, individual employees CareerSource determines eligible may qualify for WIOA Individual Training Account vouchers, which pay the training provider directly and may reduce or eliminate out-of-pocket cost. Eligibility is determined by CareerSource and is not guaranteed. CareerSource Tampa Bay administers ITA vouchers for residents of Hillsborough and Pinellas Counties. The official eligibility requirements page lists all qualifying criteria including residency age citizenship and income thresholds.

4
The ETPL Approval Process

The Eligible Training Provider List is the official state approved list of training institutions authorized to receive WIOA funding. All institutions must be licensed by the Florida Commission for Independent Education before applying. CGI's application is filed and under review. The three-step process takes providers from CIE licensure through state ETPL registration to local CareerSource approval.
